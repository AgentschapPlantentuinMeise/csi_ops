# **Standard Operating Procedure – Sample Preparation for Acid Digestion via Sample Disruption using a *QIAGEN TissueLyser III* ball mill**

# 1\. Introduction

This standard operating procedure (SOP) describes a procedure for sample disruption using the *QIAGEN TissueLyser III* ball mill. The TissueLyser III provides high-speed shaking with oscillation frequencies of 3–30 Hz (180−1800 oscillations per minute). A known weight of the sample is placed in the PTFE (Teflon) grinding jar and a PTFE Grinding Ball is added. The sample and the grinding jar can be frozen in liquid nitrogen, or at room temperature[^1] prior to sample disruption. The sample is then simultaneously disrupted and homogenized by high-speed shaking (*bead milling*). The resulting powder/crumbs are then quantitatively transferred into a plastic weighing dish with a plastic spoon as a feedstock for subsequent acid digestion. Note that the sample can only come in contact with plastic (e.g. Teflon) materials to avoid metal contamination.

# 2\. Scope

This procedure may be applied to any organic sample that is amenable to sample disruption. This includes several commodities like coffee, soybeans, cocoa beans and timber.

## 6.5 Before starting sample disruption

- Always wear **safety glasses**, **gloves**, and a **lab coat.**  
- Always check the **power cord** and the **plug** for damage before usage.  
- If liquid nitrogen is handled, always wear isolation gloves to avoid frostbite injuries.  
- The low temperature of liquid nitrogen can change the mechanical properties of materials. **Nitrogen cooled plastic vessels** can become **brittle** and break when used in the TissueLyser III.  
  * Only use liquid nitrogen with vessels that have been **cleared** for such use in the TissueLyser III. This is the case for the grinding jar set from QIAGEN.  
- **Avoid spilling water or chemicals** onto the TissueLyser III as this may cause damage. Avoid spills by **always keeping vessels upright**.

## 6.6 During Sample Disruption

**Do not operate** the TissueLyser III **with any covers or parts removed**.

**Do not leave** the TissueLyser III **unattended** during operation.

The sound level may be high depending on the type of material, the number of Grinding Balls used, the set grinding frequency, and the grinding time. Excess noise in terms of intensity and duration can lead to **impairments or permanent damage to hearing**. Consider wearing hearing protection.

If **liquid** has spilled inside the TissueLyser III, switch off the TissueLyser III, disconnect it from the power outlet, and contact QIAGEN Technical Services.

Depending on the sample and the disruption parameters, grinding jars and **samples can heat up considerably** during the disruption process. Only touch the grinding jars with protective gloves and wait for equilibration to room temperature before opening the jars.

# 7\. Sample Disruption

## 7.1 Preparing the Sample in the grinding jar

1) For tough samples  
   1) Take 0,3-2,0 g of sample per jar.  
   2) Ensure that both grinding jars are of similar weight.  
   3) Do not operate the TissueLyser III with only a single grinding jar installed.  
      1) There should always be a second grinding jar containing a sample and its own grinding ball.  
2) Add the Grinding Ball to both grinding jars.  
3) Fit the cap upright on the grinding jar and screw fingertight.  
   1) Make sure to not screw slantwise as this may damage the inner thread.  
4) In **general**:  
- Plant tissues and lyophilized tissues can be disrupted and homogenized at room temperature without a lysis buffer.   
- Alternatively, most fresh tissues can be disrupted with a lysis buffer at room temperature.  
- If previous methods do not work or if no lysis buffer is available,  fresh or frozen tissues can be disrupted without a lysis buffer if the grinding jar containing the sample is frozen in liquid nitrogen for 1 min.

  - Lysis buffer must not be added if processing frozen grinding jar sets.

- To acquire a grain size of **\<250 μm**:

  - **Cocoa** can be grinded with pestle and mortar (main lab).

  - **Roasted coffee beans** can be disrupted at room temperature.

  - **Timber** can be disrupted after freezing at \-70 °C (room 223).

  - **Green coffee and soy beans** can be disrupted after freezing with liquid N2.

5) Freezing with liquid N2 goes as follows:   
1) Collect the liquid N2 from stock with the thermos with the help of the insulated gloves (*Figure 4*).

   ![][image6]

   *Figure 4: Retrieving liquid N2 from stock with insulated gloves and in a thermos.*

2) Place the grinding jars containing the sample in the insulated box and pour the liquid N2 onto the jars and into the box.  
   1) You will notice the liquid will start bubbling when in contact with the jars.

3) Slowly turn over and roll the jars with flask thongs (like sausages on a grill) so they are frozen homogenically (*Figure 5*).

   ![][image7]

		*Figure 5: Rolling around the jars  with a flask thong.*

4) When the liquid stops bubbling, both the jars and the liquid are about the same temperature and the jars are ready for disruption. 

5) If the liquid nitrogen is not needed anymore, let the liquid nitrogen evaporate.

## 7.2 Attaching the grinding jar set

Attach a TissueLyser grinding jar set to the TissueLyser III as follows:

1. To release the locking device (If the locking pin isn’t already unlocked);  
   1. Pull the locking pin (A) upward out of its slot, and rotate it by 90° (*Figure 6*)  
2. Turn the handwheel (B) counterclockwise until the maximum clamping range is reached.   
3. Insert the grinding jar, and press it lightly into the indentation in the clamp (C).  
4. Remove the insulation gloves if necessary.  
   1. Do not touch the frozen grinding jars in order to avoid frostbite injuries.  
5. Rotate back the locking pin (A) by 90° until it engages with its slot.   
6. Turn the handwheel (B) clockwise with two fingers until the grinding jar is seated in the clamp and level and does not move freely. 

7. Continue to rotate the handwheel clockwise until six to eight easily audible clicks are heard; the locking pin (A) rises and falls with each audible click. 

   *Figure 6:  Left-hand clamp of the TissueLyser III. **A** Locking pin. **B** Handwheel. **C** Indentation in clamp.*

7.3 Running an experiment   
To run a disruption protocol:

1) Switch on the instrument at the main power switch on the back on the right side.  
   1) The display turns on and the rotary knob is illuminated (*Figure 7*).  
   2) The instrument is controlled with the integrated touchscreen located on top of the instrument in combination with the rotary knob. The different symbols and their meanings are described in Appendix A.

      ![][image8]

      *Figure 7: Display and illuminated rotary knob.*

2) Open and close the instrument once to initiate the safety sensor. The instrument is now ready for usage.

3) Choose one of the following methods to disrupt the sample:    
- Direct parameter entry on the home screen (*Figure 8, A*).  
  - Disruption time: from 10 seconds up to a maximum of 8 hours(*Figure 8, E*).  
  - Oscillation frequency: can be adjusted in steps of 1 Hz (*Figure 8, F*).  
  - The disruption parameters for a particular application can be stored in the memory of the *TissueLyser III* as a program (Figure 8, B) or a program cycle (Figure 8, C).  
  - For recommended settings, see below.  
- Selection of a disruption program.  
- Selection of a disruption program cycle.

  ![][image9]

  *Figure 8: **A** Home **B** Programs **C** Program Cycles **D** System Settings **E** Disruption time **F** Disruption.*

  Keep in mind that grinding jars and samples can heat up considerably during the disruption process. This is why rest periods should be added if creating a new program cycle.

**Recommended settings** are as follows:

- 2 min at 30 Hz: for **roasted coffee beans.**  
- 2 min at 30 Hz: for **timber** that has been frozen at \-70 °C.  
- 1 min at 30 Hz: for **green coffee** and **soy beans** that have been frozen in liquid N2\.

4) Press the “START” key on the touchscreen to run the disruption process with the specified settings.

   1) If a program cycle with pause is run.

      1) The instrument will automatically pause the run after half of the cycles.

      2) The run will only continue the disruption when “START” is pressed again.

   2) Listen carefully for changes in sound as these may indicate that the grinding jars are not properly attached.

      1) In case of not properly attached grinding jars: Stop the disruption process and take out the grinding jars and reattach them.

| \! To pause the TissueLyser III before reaching the end of the run:: Press the “PAUSE” key on the keypad. To continue operation of the TissueLyser III from the time remaining: Press the “START” key. To cancel operation of the TissueLyser III: Press the “STOP” key. When the TissueLyser III is no longer in operation or in case of emergency, switch it off with the power switch. |
| :---- |

5) Remove the grinding jars (with protective gloves) when the run is finished:  
   1) Lift the locking pin (A) and turn the handwheel (B) in the opposite direction.  
   2) If the locking pin cannot be pulled upward to release it:  
      1) Turn the handwheel slightly clockwise: the locking pin should then be freely moveable.  
      2) DO NOT FORCE it upward using a hammer or other tool, as damage may occur.  
6) Wait for equilibration to room temperature before opening the jars.   
7) Transfer the content with the use of a plastic spoon into a plastic weighing dish and proceed according to the task.  
   1) Use different plastic spoons (or clean them with MilliQ water) and weigh dishes for different samples to avoid cross-contamination.  
8) To remove any left-over residue present in the grinding jars and grinding balls:  
   1) Use a pipet tip to scrape the sample into the weighing dish but.  
   2) Use different plastic pipet tips for different samples to avoid cross-contamination.  
9) Clean and dry the grinding jar set and TissueLyser III instrument and inspect all parts for damage	 as described in *8\. Cleaning, maintenance and storage.*

# 8\. Cleaning, maintenance and storage

Perform the following regular maintenance procedures (i.e. after each daily use) to ensure reliable operation of the TissueLyser III. 

## 8.1 Cleaning

### 8.1.1 TissueLyser III

For cleaning the TissueLyser III apparatus, following aspect must be followed or taken into consideration:

- If solvents or saline, acidic, or alkaline solutions are spilt on the TissueLyser III, wipe them away immediately.  
- Only perform cleaning with liquids after disconnecting the instrument from the power grid.   
- The TissueLyser III should be wiped after use using a **soft cloth** moistened with a suitable cleaning agent (e.g. **Ethanol, 70%** for general cleaning).  
  * Use liquids only on the cloth.  
    * Never use free-flowing liquids (Do not use spray bottles to spray cleaning or disinfectant liquids onto surfaces of the TissueLyser III).  
    * **DO NOT USE BLEACH** to clean or disinfect used labware.  
      * Bleach in contact with salts from the used buffers can produce toxic fumes.  
  * To remove residual cleaning agent and avoid instrument damage:  
    * wiped immediately the residual cleaning with a soft cloth moistened with water. 

### 8.1.2 Grinding jar set

To clean the grinding jar and grinding balls set (after each use) follow the following steps:

1. Remove any stubborn stains.  
2. Rinse 1x **with DI water** (left sink).  
3. Spray bottles can be used for the following rinse steps:  
   1. Rinse **3x with MilliQ** **water.**  
   2. Rinse **1x with Ethanol (\<70%).**  
      1. Catch the ethanol in a separate waste container (Figure 9).  
4. **Dry the grinding jar set** with a paper towel.  
5. **DO NOT** autoclave the grinding jar set.  
6. The Grinder Jar set must be dry before it is reused.

   

 *Figure 9: Catch the ethanol rinse water.*

## 8.2 Maintenance

### 8.2.1 TissueLyser III

After each maintenance, write the data, type of maintenance and any remark down in table 3 (see appendix III "Maintenance journal”).  
If the TissueLyser III is not in use, switch it off at the power switch. If the TissueLyser III will not be used for a prolonged period of time, unplug the power cord from the power outlet.

**Every 6 months**, **the limit switch and the brake** should be checked for correct operation.

To check the limit switch and the brake:

1. Install a TissueLyser grinding jar set in the instrument according to instructions.  
2. Switch on the TissueLyser III.  
3. Set the oscillation frequency to 30 Hz.  
4. Press the “START” key.  
5. Raise the hood.  
   1. The drive motor should switch itself off, and “E50 SECURITY CIRCUIT” should appear in the display.  
6. Lower the hood.  
   1. The drive motor should not switch itself on again.  
   2. If there is a defect, contact QIAGEN.

Replacement of the fuses:

1. Pull the power cord.  
   1. Replacement of the fuses without removal of the power cord can lead to **life threatening electrical shock**.  
   2. For the procedure of how to replace the fuses, please refer to the User Manual.

### 8.2.2 Grinding jar set

TissueLyser grinding jar sets may become worn, depending on the frequency of use and the samples disrupted. This may lead to damage to the clamps of the TissueLyser III. Check the TissueLyser grinding jar sets regularly for wear and tear, and replace if necessary.

# 9\. Waste disposal 

Any non-hazardous organic material that has not been in contact with chemicals can be disposed of in the sink.

Recipients containing liquid nitrogen should not be disposed of in the sink to prevent spreading in the sewers. Use a dedicated waste bottle or let the liquid nitrogen evaporate.

Ethanol rinse water should be disposed of into a special ethanol waste container (present in the right fume hood).
