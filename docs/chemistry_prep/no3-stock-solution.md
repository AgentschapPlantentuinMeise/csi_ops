# SOP 01 — Preparation of 5% Nitrate (NO₃⁻) Stock Solution

## 1. Purpose
To describe the safe and reproducible preparation of a **5% (w/v) nitrate stock solution** using **65% nitric acid (HNO₃)** diluted with Milli‑Q water. This stock is used for calibration, matrix matching, and general analytical workflows.

## 2. Scope
Applicable to all CSI laboratory personnel preparing nitrate solutions for ICP‑OES, colorimetric assays, or general wet‑chemistry procedures.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Analysts**: Prepare the solution according to this SOP and document batch details.
- **Lab coordinator**: Verify labeling, expiry, and storage compliance.
- **Quality officer**: Review preparation logs during audits.

## 4. Safety and Hazards

> **DANGER — Nitric Acid (65%)**
> - Strong oxidizer  
> - Corrosive to skin, eyes, and respiratory tract  
> - Reacts violently with organics and reducing agents  
> - Always add **acid to water**, never the reverse  

Required PPE:
- Lab coat, splash‑proof goggles, acid‑resistant gloves (nitrile + over‑gloves), face shield for bulk handling  
- Work in a certified fume hood  

Refer to the CSI Hazard Sheet: `sop/sample-preparation/hazards.md`.

---

## 5. Materials and Equipment
- 65% HNO₃ (analytical grade)
- Milli‑Q water (≥18.2 MΩ·cm)
- Volumetric flask (500 mL or 1 L)
- Acid‑resistant pipettes or glass pipette + pipette filler
- HDPE or Teflon storage bottle (acid‑compatible)
- Label with batch ID, date, preparer, expiry

---

## 6. Definitions
**5% (w/v)** = 5 g nitrate per 100 mL final solution.  
When prepared from nitric acid, this corresponds to a defined dilution of 65% HNO₃ to achieve the equivalent nitrate mass fraction.

---

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Preparation of 500 mL 5% NO₃⁻ Stock Solution

1. Place a **500 mL volumetric flask** in the fume hood.  
2. Add approximately **300 mL Milli‑Q water** to the flask.  
3. Using an acid‑resistant pipette, add **3.8 mL of 65% HNO₃** to the water.  
   - This volume corresponds to the nitrate mass needed for a 5% (w/v) equivalent solution.  
4. Swirl gently to mix.  
5. Allow the solution to cool if any heat is generated.  
6. Fill to the **500 mL mark** with Milli‑Q water.  
7. Invert the flask several times to homogenize.

### 7.2 Preparation of 1 L 5% NO₃⁻ Stock Solution (recommended for high‑throughput labs)

1. Place a **1 L volumetric flask** in the fume hood.  
2. Add approximately **600 mL Milli‑Q water**.  
3. Add **7.6 mL of 65% HNO₃** to the water.  
4. Mix gently and allow to cool.  
5. Fill to the **1 L mark** with Milli‑Q water.  
6. Invert to homogenize.

> **Note:** The 1 L preparation reduces batch‑to‑batch variability and is recommended when calibration standards are prepared daily.

---

## 8. Labeling
Label the stock bottle with:
- **“5% NO₃⁻ Stock Solution”**
- Concentration: 5% (w/v) nitrate equivalent  
- Source: 65% HNO₃  
- Batch ID (YYYYMMDD‑XX)  
- Preparer initials  
- Preparation date  
- Expiry date (see below)

---

## 9. Storage and Stability
- Store in **HDPE or Teflon** bottle  
- Keep at **room temperature** in a chemical cabinet  
- Protect from light  
- **Expiry: 3 months** from preparation (unless QC indicates earlier degradation)

---

## 10. Quality Control
- Check clarity (no precipitates, no discoloration)  
- Verify pH < 1 (typical for nitric acid dilutions)  
- Record batch in the CSI Reagent Log  
- Cross‑reference with SOP 03 (CRM QC) when used for calibration workflows

---

## 11. Documentation
Record the following in the Reagent Preparation Log:
- Batch ID  
- Volume prepared  
- Acid lot number  
- Preparer  
- Date  
- QC observations  
- Expiry date
